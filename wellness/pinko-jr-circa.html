☀️ THE REAL SCIENCE — Tap to learn

Biology: Your body has an internal clock called the suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN) — it runs everything: cortisol, melatonin, hunger, alertness.

How it works: Light hits melanopsin in your eyes → tells brain what time it is → sets your whole day.

Dr. HubermanExplains: Light → Cortisol → alertness

🌅 WAKE UP: Light within 30 minutes

WHY: Cortisol Awakening Response (CAR) peaks 30 min after waking. Get bright light in your eyes = max alertness for the day.

The habit: Daylight, NOT sunglasses. Even cloudy day = 10,000 lux. Indoor = 300 lux. Big difference.

Natural sunlight ideal. If impossible, use 10k lux light box within 30 min of waking.

☕ MORNING: No caffeine for 90 min

WHY: Adenosine (sleep debt) builds overnight. Caffeine blocks the receptor but doesn't remove the debt — it just masks it.

Caffeine before 90 min post-wake = afternoon crash.

Rule: Wake at 5:30am → first咖啡 at 7:00am minimum.

💪 EXERCISE: Before 4pm

WHY: Exercise raises cortisol. Good in day, TERRIBLE at night.

Optimal: 12-3pm (peak body temperature, testosterone). Evening workouts = ruined sleep.

Cutoff: 4pm hard stop for intense exercise.

🚫 CAFFEINE CUTOFF: 2pm (8 hours before sleep)

WHY: Caffeine half-life = 5-6 hours.

200mg at 2pm → 100mg at 8pm → 50mg at 2am.

If you sleep at 10pm: NO caffeine after 2pm.

😴 SLEEP: Cool + Dark + Consistent

Temperature: Body drops 1-2°F to sleep. Bedroom = 65-68°F.

Darkness: Melatonin rises in dark. Blackout curtains essential.

Consistency: Same sleep/wake time ± 30min = circadian entrainment. Weekend drift = Monday blues.

📵 NIGHT: No blue light after 8pm

WHY: Blue light (446nm) suppresses melatonin MORE than any other wavelength.

Red/amber lights = fine. Blue = destroy sleep.

Solution: f.lux on devices, or red bulb, or books.
